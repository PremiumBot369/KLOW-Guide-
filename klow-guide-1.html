Research Timeline

Protocol Timeframe

Expected research observations based on community and preclinical data. Individual results in research models vary.

Week 1–2
Tolerance Assessment — 5 units / 5× weekly
Begin at half the standard dose to assess tolerance. Observe for any injection site sensitivity or systemic response. GHK-Cu may produce a subtle flushing response in some subjects during initial exposure. No significant regenerative effects expected at this stage.
Week 3–4
Loading Phase — 10 units / 5× weekly
Step up to the standard 10-unit dose. BPC-157 and TB-500 begin supporting tissue repair signalling. KPV's anti-inflammatory action via NF-κB inhibition begins modulating inflammatory markers. Early observations in repair models typically emerge around week 3–4.
Week 5–8
Peak Loading — 10 units / 5× weekly
Primary phase for tissue regeneration observations. GHK-Cu drives collagen synthesis and extracellular matrix remodelling. BPC-157 supports angiogenesis via VEGF upregulation. Research models typically show the most pronounced tissue repair markers during this window.
Week 9–12
Maintenance Phase — 10 units / 3× weekly
Reduce frequency to 3× weekly to consolidate gains and reduce androgen receptor fatigue from sustained GHK-Cu exposure. Continued anti-aging and anti-inflammatory observations. Begin planning cycle-off timing.
Week 12+ — Cycle Off
Rest Period — 4–8 weeks off
Complete cessation of the blend for 4–8 weeks. Allows receptor sensitivity to reset, particularly for GHK-Cu's copper-mediated pathways. Bloodwork recommended to assess copper/zinc balance before restarting. After rest, the cycle can be repeated from the loading phase.

Step-by-Step

Reconstitution Instructions

01

Gather Equipment

You will need: KLOW 80mg vial, bacteriostatic water (BAC water), 3mL sterile syringe for reconstitution, U-100 insulin syringes for dosing, alcohol prep swabs, and a sharps container.

02

Sterilise Rubber Stoppers

Wipe the rubber stopper on the BAC water vial with an alcohol swab. Allow to air dry for 10–15 seconds. Repeat for the KLOW peptide vial. Use a fresh swab for each — never cross-contaminate.

03

Draw Bacteriostatic Water

Draw 2mL of BAC water into your reconstitution syringe (recommended volume — see table above for alternatives). First inject an equal volume of air into the BAC water vial to equalise pressure, making drawing easier.

04

Inject Into Peptide Vial

Insert the needle into the KLOW vial at a slight angle. Aim the stream at the inside glass wall — not directly at the powder. Slowly depress the plunger. Never force the water directly onto the lyophilised cake.

05

Dissolve — Never Shake

Gently swirl the vial in slow circular motions until the powder is fully dissolved. The solution should appear clear to very slightly blue-tinted (from the copper in GHK-Cu). Do not shake — this can degrade peptide structure.

06

Label & Refrigerate

Label the vial with the reconstitution date. Store immediately in the refrigerator at 2–8°C. Reconstituted KLOW is stable for 4–6 weeks with BAC water when refrigerated. Do not freeze reconstituted vials — ice crystals damage peptide structure.
